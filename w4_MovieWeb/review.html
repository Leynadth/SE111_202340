Review of The Batman

LEYNADTH SOSA August 10, 2023

★★★★★

In the dark and brooding streets of Gotham City, a new era of darkness and intrigue dawns as "The Batman" takes center stage. Directed by visionary filmmaker Matt Reeves, this thrilling reimagining of the iconic vigilante follows a young and tormented Bruce Wayne, brilliantly portrayed by Robert Pattinson, as he navigates the fine line between justice and vengeance. As corruption and chaos grip the city, a formidable gallery of villains emerges, challenging the Caped Crusader's resolve in ways he never anticipated. Prepare to embark on a riveting journey into the depths of Gotham's shadows, where a conflicted hero rises to become the symbol of hope the city so desperately needs.

At the heart of the film is Robert's portrayal of Bruce Wayne, which stands out for its depth and complexity. The actor masterfully navigates between Bruce's public persona and his private struggles, revealing a young and tormented hero haunted by his past. This more vulnerable and emotionally raw version of Batman adds layers of relatability, allowing the audience to connect with his inner turmoil on a deeper level. As Bruce investigates the escalating crime wave in Gotham, we witness his journey towards embracing his vigilante alter ego, Batman. This development is presented in a grounded and realistic manner, which makes it all the more compelling.

Reeves' direction shines in the film's visual style, particularly in its portrayal of Gotham City. The city itself becomes a character, oozing with a gothic and gritty ambiance that reflects the pervasive corruption and decay that plague its streets. The meticulous attention to detail in the production design, coupled with atmospheric cinematography, creates an immersive world that fully draws viewers into the dark and enigmatic setting. The action sequences, while not as frequent as in some other superhero films, are well-choreographed and intense, emphasizing Batman's resourcefulness and physical prowess.

The rogues' gallery of villains that Batman encounters adds another layer of intrigue to the narrative. Each antagonist is portrayed with a sense of depth and motivation that transcends mere caricature. The standout performance comes from Paul Dano, who portrays the Riddler with a chilling and captivating intensity. This portrayal offers a fresh perspective on the character's motivations and origins, making it one of the film's most compelling arcs.

Trivia and fun facts about The Batman

  • The first live-action portrayal of Batman with eye makeup
  • Colin Farrell stated that he went to Starbucks to try out his prosthetics and makeup for the Penguin for the first time. Nobody recognized him, but he got a couple of stares from people.
  • Matt Reeves stated that his version of the Riddler was partly inspired by the infamous serial killer Zodiac who operated in California in the late 1960s.
